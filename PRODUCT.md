# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Arabic-speaking Egyptian consumers — primarily aged 20–45 — who have a nostalgic connection to old Cairo, Egyptian street culture, and classic family aesthetics. They browse on mobile, respond to warm emotional storytelling, and value authenticity over generic luxury. They want to feel proud of choosing something distinctly Egyptian rather than a pale imitation of western brands.

Secondary audience: diaspora Egyptians and Arabic-speaking customers across the Middle East who feel the pull of cultural heritage.

## Product Purpose

Robabikia is a luxury Egyptian brand selling perfumes, contemporary-classic clothing, and leather shoes. The name evokes the street vendors of old Cairo — a portal to collective memory. Every product is positioned as a handcrafted story, not just a purchase.

The experience should feel like opening your grandmother's wardrobe: rich scents, textured fabrics, and the weight of real things. Orders are fulfilled across all Egyptian governorates with cash-on-delivery. WhatsApp is the primary human touchpoint.

Success looks like: a visitor feeling the brand's soul before they read a single price tag.

## Brand Personality

الأصالة، الحنين، الفخامة — Authenticity, Nostalgia, Luxury.

Voice: warm, poetic, proud. Arabic-first. The brand speaks in the Egyptian dialect used in the story section — intimate and direct, not formal or stiff. English is a secondary label, never the primary voice.

Emotional goals: evoke cultural pride, create a sense of belonging to something genuine, inspire quiet confidence.

## Anti-references

- **Western luxury minimalism**: Cream backgrounds, Didot serifs, Swiss grid restraint. This reads as foreign and impersonal for a brand rooted in Cairo's bazaars and old-world richness.
- **Generic Arabic e-commerce**: Noon.com / Jumia-style utility — cluttered, price-forward, functional but soulless. Robabikia is the opposite of a marketplace.

## Design Principles

1. **Heritage before trend**: Every visual decision should feel like it belongs to old Cairo reimagined, not imported from a western luxury template.
2. **The scent is the story**: Product presentation is immersive, not transactional. The product page is an experience, not a spec sheet.
3. **Gold earns its place**: The gold accent is not decoration — it marks what matters. Use it with restraint so it retains authority.
4. **Arabic leads**: RTL is the default experience. English exists as a courtesy, not a primary voice.
5. **Warmth over coolness**: When in doubt between two design directions, choose the one that feels warmer, more textured, more human.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline: 4.5:1 text contrast ratio, keyboard navigation for primary flows, screen reader support for interactive elements, meaningful alt text on product images.
